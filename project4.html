We have been working on a project focused on extracting and analyzing structured data from documents. The goal is to automate the recognition and classification of key information while ensuring accuracy through validation steps.

To achieve this, we trained a model capable of recognizing document types and extracting relevant fields. The training process involved carefully preparing labeled datasets with structured annotations. The model was fine-tuned to identify different types of documents and their respective fields.

We integrated an OCR system to extract raw text from documents, ensuring that textual content is available for further analysis. The extracted text is then processed and used for comparison with the model's predictions.

To validate the accuracy of the extracted data, we implemented a comparison mechanism. This checks whether the model's extracted values appear in the raw OCR text, helping to identify inconsistencies and improve performance.

We also separated different components into dedicated pipelines. One part of the system focuses on classification and structured extraction, while another handles direct text analysis. This modular approach improves efficiency and flexibility.

Several improvements were made to handle errors, including automatic correction of incomplete outputs and adjustments in data formatting. These refinements help ensure that the system remains reliable across different document types.

Currently, we are refining the overall accuracy and optimizing the recognition of more challenging fields. Future improvements will focus on enhancing validation processes, refining training data, and improving handling of complex document structures.
